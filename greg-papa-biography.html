Who is Greg Papa?

Greg Papa is an American sportscaster working for a football team San Francisco 49ers.

He was born Gregory Charles Papa born on 10 Oct 1962, in Buffalo, New York, US.

His ethnicity is Caucasian and had a brother named Gary Papa. Gary was born in 1954 and died in 2009.

His father’s name is Frank who passed away in 2019. Regarding his mother, he has not disclosed her name.

Regarding education, he graduated from Syracuse University.

Greg Papa: Professional Life, Career

Greg Papa started his career after completing of graduation in 1984. He was a member of the Indiana Pacers’ television and radio broadcasting team from 1984 to 1986. Then in 1986, he joined the radio announcer for the Golden State Warriors.

In 1997 he became the lead announcer on the San Antonio Spurs telecasts.

Similarly, in 2008, he had hosted 35 of the 50 scheduled editions of “Giants Pregame Live” and all 65 of the “Giants Postgame Live” shows on Comcast SportsNet Bay Area.

In the 2011-12 season, along with Gary St. Jean, he has hosted both “Warriors Pregame Live” and “Warriors Postgame Live” on CSN Bay Area.

Commentary Team

Greg was first involved with Indiana Pacers (1984–86) and then Golden State Warriors (1986–97), Oakland Athletics (1991–2003), San Antonio Spurs (1997–2000), and Oakland Raiders (1997–2018).

He was associated with San Francisco Giants from 2004 to 2008. He is now with a football team called San Francisco 49ers which he joined in 2019.
