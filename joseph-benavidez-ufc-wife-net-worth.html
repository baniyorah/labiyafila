Joseph Benavidez’s boxing career

On 3 December 2008, he was signed by US-based World Extreme Cagefighiting. Then he fought Danny Martinez and won by unanimous decision at UFC 27. He faced Jeff Curran at WEC 40 on 5 April 2009.

Afterward, he fought Dominick Cruz for the WEC Bantamweight Championship at WEC 50 on 18 August 2010.

After, he faced Demetrious Johnson at UFC 152 for the inaugural UFC Flyweight Championship on 22 September 2012. Besides, he faced Ian McCall at UFC 156 on 2 February 2013.

On 22 November 2014, he faced at UFC Fight Night 57 Dustin Ortiz and he won the back-and-forth fight via unanimous.

Besides, he was linked to a bout with former Olympic Gold Medalist Henry Cejudo at UFC 191 on 5 September 2015.

The UFC proclaimed that Benavidez would be one of the best coaches in May 2016 and opposite Henry Cejudo on the Ultimate Fighter 24 in May.

He is now for the vacant UFC Flyweight Championship set to the Deiveson Figueiredo on 29 February 2020 at UFC Fight Night 169.

He is expected to face at UFC 259 with Askar Askarov on 6 March 2021.
